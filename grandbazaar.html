Overview

Grand Bazaar is envisaged to be eastern India’s largest commercial hub. The logic behind this project was to create a win-win synergy by combining the advantages of `mandi culture`, i.e accumulated trade within a defined boundary and the modern day mall culture of the convenience of infrastructure and shopping experience. The already sold out project meets the need of the trader community for a modern working space with minimal op-ex that will be ready by mid 2019. The construction was started in January 2017 and, in a record period of 2 years, is already in its finishing stage.

Amenities

● Dedicated Air Conditioned and non-air conditioned buses plying to and fro from Grand Bazaar to important locations in Cuttack and Bhubaneswar.

● Hop-on-Hop-Off Buses to enable movement within the Grand Bazaar Campus.

● Well-Planned waiting lounges equipped with TV’s, refreshments and toilet facilities.

● Guest Houses to accommodate Business Associates.

● A Food Court offering multi-cuisine fare at reasonable prices.

● An economical and independent canteen to serve meals for the support staff.

● A separate waiting area for drivers and equipped with a parking announcement system.

● Banks, ATM’s, Medical Dispensary and Courier Services.

● A 5,000 square feet community hall.

● Modern warehousing facility offering rentable rack space.

● A sprawling underground car parking facility.

● Top-of-the line electrical and plumbing systems, power backup facilities, fire fighting system/CCTV monitoring systems and security services and public announcement systems.

● Planned Advertisements and shop signage to maintain aesthetic uniformity.

● Professional facility managers for maintenance.

● Separate quarters for security guards.

Location Advantage

Grand Bazaar is located on NH-5 between Cuttack and Bhubaneswar. NH 5 is a major National Highway in India and a part of the Golden Quadrilateral running through Odisha, Andhra Pradesh, and Tamil Nadu, over a distance of 1,533 kms. This 200-feet road will be broadened to 300 feet and will be the lifeline feeding the Grand Bazaar. Providing a congestion-free access enjoyed by no other market in the city.
